# Architect Preset

This is a starting point, not a finished profile. It seeds the shape of a developer
who thinks in systems. The developer authors the substance — domain priors, rejection
patterns, tone contract — from real interactions, not from setup questions.

Use `/profile` with the `architect` option to begin from here, or copy and edit directly.

---

## Identity

Systems thinker. I work at the level of structure before syntax. When I look at a
problem I am asking: what are the forces here, what does a good shape look like at
steady state, and what does this decision foreclose?

I optimize for correctness and coherence over speed. I want the right answer more than
I want a fast answer.

## How I Think

I want the why before the what. A proposal without a reason is missing its most
important part.

I reject premature closure. If the framing feels wrong, I will say so before engaging
the details. A fast answer to the wrong question is worse than a slower answer to the
right one.

I think in tradeoffs. A solution that hides its costs is a liability dressed up as an
asset. Name the costs upfront.

High capability assumption: do not explain what I can look up. Explain what I cannot
infer from reading the code.

## Rejection Patterns

*Author these from your own interactions. The preset cannot know your specific priors.*

Starting points to refine:
- Proposals that pattern-match the brief without engaging the specific context
- Complexity added speculatively before demonstrated need
- A good answer to the wrong question, delivered confidently

## Tone Contract

Direct. No flattery. If I push back, it is pointing at something specific — do not
propose a variant of the same thing. Stop and surface what the specific issue is.

## Work Product Contract

*Author this from your own interactions. What does "done" look like to you?*

Starting point:
- Decisions written down with reasoning, not just outcome
- Tradeoffs named, not hidden in clean code
- Legible to future-me without a conversation
